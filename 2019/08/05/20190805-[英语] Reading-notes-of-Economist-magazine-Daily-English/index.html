Reading notes of 'Economist magazine' (Daily English)

发表于 2019-08-05 | 更新于: 2019-08-13   |   更新于 2019-08-13 | 分类于 英语 |
字数统计: 605 字 | 阅读时长 ≈ 3 分钟

ABSTRACT: I am writing this essay to record the key points of three articles which are published on the “China” section in “Economist 20190803”.

  • Jobless graduates / Skilled but jobless / Idle hands
  • Hong Kong’s protests / Reclaiming Hong Kong / Protest, but no movement
  • Chaguan The semiotics（n. 符号学） of meeting rooms / Meeting room politics / Armchair warriors

1. Idle hands

China’s economy, influenced by the trade war with America, is growing at its slowest pace in nearly 30 years. Meanwhile, Chinese universities contributed 8.3m graduates this summer, nearly 1.5 times as many as ten years ago. In addition, the retrenchment of visa policies in weastern countries will foster the returning of more oversea students. It’s safe to say that the job application of Chinese graduates this year is going to much tougher than ever before.

Fortunately, guaranteeing the employment rate is the first priority of government, for the sake of social stability. Chinese government have issued many policies to stimulate the recruitment of the enterprises. But inevitablely, the proporation of fresh graduates who have found full-time jobs within 6 months of graduation have fallen around 4% since 2014, and the average monthly salary for new graduates also fell largely from 2015 to 2017.

Though the employment situation is much more worse, it only brings threats to those who are not well skilled. We have reasons to believe that as long as one is skilled and ready, there will be chance for him/her.

2. Protest, but no movement

Nowadays, Hong Kong is experiencing an unprecedent riot which have paralysed the transportation, business and routine life of Hong Kong. The central government have reiterated its unchangeable stance toward Hong Kong issues. The local garrison（n. / vt. 卫戍，驻守）of the People’s Liberation Army (PLA) also announce its confidence of coping with the severe situation in Hong Kong.

As what my friends sees and hears, the demonstration kept mounting up and evoking numerous riots, which interrupted the regular order of Hong Kong largely. While in the article of “Economist”, it should support the demonstration and condemn the government for lacking sincerity about process of electing chief executive freely.

From where I stand, I indeed hope that Hong Kong can return to peace and prosperous. The apeal for general election is more likely to be used and provoked by capitalism and politicians. What is the priority we need to do, I guess, is reading more and thinking more.

3. Chaguan: Armchair warriors</font>

Chaguan is a special column of China section in ‘Economist’, just named after a classical novel in China mainland. In the famous book, the author ‘Laoshe’ described the decrepit process of an old Beijing teahouse between 1920s and 1940s. More importantly, it reflected ups and downs of the whole modern society of China. Analogously, the special column is aiming to point out problems of China and bring out corresponding tactics.

This article talked about the armchair used at the occasions of the reception meeting for foreigh leaders. As it says, it is a unique phenomenon of China to hold a meeting at this kind of horseshoe pattern office. And they think it’s not only unconfortable for the occupants to sit but also inconvenient to exchange opinions with aides and companions.

Yes, there are some demerits of this kind of meeting, and China is also absorbing the experience and holding more and more bussiness pattern meetings. But there is no deny that armchair is a symbol of tranditional culture and deserves to be respected. Pragmatism is definitely right, while advocating tranditional cultures is also reasonable.
